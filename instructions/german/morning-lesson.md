# German Morning Lessons

## Student Level
**Intermediate/Advanced** - Already somewhat fluent

## Progress Tracking
Before each lesson, read `instructions/german/progress.md` to:
- Review previously covered idioms and vocabulary
- Check grammar topics already refined
- Note any marked areas for continued practice
- See which texts/sources have been analyzed

After each lesson, update `progress.md` with:
- Date and topic covered
- New vocabulary/idioms added
- Grammar refinements practiced
- Any texts analyzed
- Observations about fluency and areas to polish

## Daily Lesson Structure

When delivering the German morning lesson:

### 1. Greeting
Start with a German greeting, varying formality and regional expressions.

### 2. Advanced Vocabulary
- One nuanced word or expression
- Subtle meanings and connotations
- Register (formal/informal/colloquial)
- Regional variations if relevant (Austrian, Swiss German)

### 3. Idiom or Redewendung
- One German idiom or fixed expression
- Literal vs. actual meaning
- Usage context and examples
- Similar expressions in English if applicable

### 4. Grammar Refinement
Focus on tricky areas for advanced learners:
- Subjunctive II (Konjunktiv II) nuances
- Extended adjective constructions
- Passive voice variations
- Subtle preposition usage
- Particle verbs and their separability
- Genitive case in modern usage
- Relative clauses with complex structures

### 5. Authentic Text Snippet
- Short excerpt from news, literature, or media
- 2-3 sentences to analyze
- Discuss style, register, or interesting constructions

### 6. Speaking/Writing Prompt
- Brief discussion question or writing exercise
- Push toward more sophisticated expression

## Focus Areas for Fluent Speakers

- **Precision**: Choosing the *right* word among synonyms
- **Register**: Formal vs. informal vs. written German
- **Idiomaticity**: Sounding natural, not textbook
- **Current usage**: Modern expressions, Anglicisms, evolving language
- **Humor & wordplay**: Puns, cultural references
- **False friends**: Words that seem similar to English but differ

## Teaching Style
- Challenge without overwhelming
- Point out common mistakes even fluent speakers make
- Introduce colloquial and regional variety
- Discuss language as a living, evolving system
